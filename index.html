There has been considerable media coverage of Bitcoin (BTC) and other cryptocurrencies (including NFTs) for a number years, but the stories routinely focus on one key indicator of interest: PRICE. But what about volume?

In general, volume refers to the total value of an asset that has been bought and sold during the specified period of time. Trading volume is often analyzed in conjuction with price changes to confirm the existence, continuation or reversal of a trend. Consequently, volume indicators can provide investors with signals regarding when to enter (buy) or exit (sell) their position.

In a mature market, volume and price work together to create such signals as follows[1]:

  • Increasing volume + increasing prices:
    potential buying opportunity

  • Increasing volume + decreasing prices:
    best to stay on the sidelines

  • Declining volume + decreasing prices:
    opportunity to sell and take profits

  • Declining volume + increasing prices:
    may indicate a coming reversal and a time to buy

Bitcoin Becomes Legitimate

Although BTC and other cryptocurrencies had been around for a number of years, 2017 marks the first year BTC truly gained widespread, public attention.

It was a year marked by multiple record-setting highs (both in terms of price AND volume). The close connection between these two indicators of interest marked a strong momentum signal and

The following milestones in 2017 contributed to Bitcoin's traction: [2]

  • Major banks such as Barclays, Citi Bank and Deutsche Bank annouce they are investigating ways they might be able to work with Bitcoin.

  • Japan and Russia annouce plans to accept bitcoin as a legal payment method and legalize the use of cryptocurrencies.


"Crypto Winter"

After breaking $1,000 and swiftly soaring to highs in excess of $20K in 2017, it’s easy to understand why 2018 is commonly referred to as the “Crypto Winter” among those who follow it closely.

  • Japan's largest over-the-counter crypto marketplace is hacked and assets valued at $530 million USD are stolen.

  • South Korea bans annonymous BTC transactions.

  • Online payment company Stripe announces it will phase out support for BTC payments.
